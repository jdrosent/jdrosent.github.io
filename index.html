James D. Rosenthal

Ph.D. Candidate

University of Washington

Welcome to my website. I'm currently a Ph.D. candidate and National Science Graduate Research Fellow in the Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Washington (UW).

My dissertation research focuses on the development of wireless brain-computer interfaces, exploring how architectural innovation in embedded systems can reduce the size, weight, and power consumption of wireless systems and enable new methods for monitoring and treating neurological disorders.

Much of my work has been in novel applications of electromagnetic theory, ultra-low power wireless embedded systems, and electronic instrumentation for extreme environments. This work have contributed to the development of neural prosthetics at the UW Center for Neurotechnology and the measurement of biologically-harmful space radiation at NASA to improve aviation safety.

Outside of research, I'm passionate about outreach, mentoring, and teaching. Also lots of hobbies.

For more information, check out my CV, Publications and Projects.
