James Rosenthal

Ph.D. Candidate

University of Washington

James is a Ph.D. candidate and National Science Graduate Research Fellow in the Department of Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Washington (UW).

His dissertation research focuses on the development of wireless brain-computer interfaces, exploring how architectural innovation in embedded systems can reduce the size, weight, and power consumption of wireless systems and enable revolutionary clinical treatments for neurological disorders.

Much of his work has been in novel applications of electromagnetic theory, ultra-low power wireless embedded systems, and electronic instrumentation for extreme environments. Examples of his work have contributed to the development of neural prosthetics at the UW Center for Neurotechnology and the measurement of biologically-harmful space radiation at NASA to improve aviation safety.

In addition to research, James is actively engaged in outreach, mentoring, and teaching.

For more information, please see his CV, Publications and Projects.
